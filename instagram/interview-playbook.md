# Shop Talk — Interview Playbook

The format, questions, and pitch rules for every Shop Talk episode. Open this file before recording any guest interview.

---

## Core principle: never pitch in the conversation

The interview is the marketing. The moment you pitch StyleDesk inside the conversation, three things break:

1. The guest stops sharing the episode (they don't want to feel like a sales tool)
2. The audience stops trusting (it becomes an infomercial)
3. The series loses re-watch value

**Instead:** ask questions designed to surface phone/booking pain. Guests will describe the exact problem StyleDesk solves without you saying a word about it. The audience makes the connection on their own — 10× more powerful than any pitch.

The funnel lives **outside** the audio:
- Corner overlay (`SHOP TALK · EP 01 · STYLEDESK`)
- End card (1.5s flash: "Brought to you by StyleDesk — link in bio")
- Caption (founding offer line)
- Pinned comment ("Loved Hilda's answer to the 2pm question? StyleDesk is built for exactly that. Link in bio.")

## Visual format: faceless interview with on-screen text question cards

**The host (you) is never on camera.** Camera stays on the guest. Between answers, a beautiful brand-locked text card displays the next question, then we cut back to the guest answering. This format:

- Keeps you private (no fame, no recognition, your stated goal)
- Puts the guest in the spotlight (they're more likely to share + come back)
- Reinforces brand on every cut (gold/burgundy/Helvetica = StyleDesk visual identity)
- Looks editorial/premium (Vogue, NYT, Vox aesthetic — not amateur podcast)
- Is repeatable as a template (build the question card once, swap text per question)

### Card spec (locked from `DESIGN-SYSTEM.md`)

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Canvas | 1080 × 1920 (9:16 vertical Reel/Story format) |
| Background | Burgundy gradient `#3a0018 → #2a0012 → #1a000b` |
| Ambient glow | Top-center radial, gold `#c9a064` at 0.18 opacity |
| Top-left overlay | `SHOP TALK` (gold, weight 500) + `STYLEDESK · EP 01` (gold, weight 300) |
| Eyebrow | `QUESTION 02` — gold, tracked +6, centered |
| Question lines | Helvetica Neue / Inter, weight 200 (white) for setup, weight 500 (gold gradient) for the punchline |
| Divider | Gold line · dot · line (matches feed posts) |
| URL | `STYLEDESK.AI` — gold, tracked +8, centered |

### Example file
See **`shop-talk-question-card-example.svg`** in this folder — uses The Anchor question ("Walk me through what your phone looks like on a Saturday at 2pm.") as the demo. PNG render in `png-ready/shop-talk-question-card-example.png`.

### Workflow per episode
1. Record guest answering all questions in one sitting (audio + video)
2. Build question cards by duplicating `shop-talk-question-card-example.svg` and swapping the question text + episode number (only 4 strings change per card)
3. In CapCut: drop the video on the timeline → cut into Q&A segments → drop each card BEFORE the matching answer for ~1 second → done

### Why not put your face in
A faceless format would normally be a growth ceiling, but for StyleDesk it's actually a feature:
- B2B/SaaS audiences buy on utility, not personality
- Brand is more sellable later (no founder dependency)
- Guests are MORE willing to come on when they're the only face
- The locked design system already does the brand-recognition work that a face usually does

The non-negotiables to make faceless work: excellent audio (DJI Mic 2), strong b-roll of the guest's shop/work to cut to, and this card system carrying the visual brand on every transition.

## The format: spine + spice

Every episode has 3 signature questions ("spine") asked in the same order, plus 5–7 questions chosen from a bank ("spice"). Spine builds brand recognition and clip-worthy moments; spice keeps episodes fresh.

### The spine — every episode, every guest

#### 🎙️ Opener
> *"In 60 seconds — who are you, where do you work, what's your specialty?"*

**Why:** Lets the guest shine immediately, gets pleasantries done fast. Audience decides in 60 seconds whether they're staying.

#### 🎙️ The Anchor *(your trademark question)*
> *"Walk me through what your phone looks like on a Saturday at 2pm."*

**Why:**
- Specific moment = concrete answer (not abstract "tell me about phones")
- Every guest will describe missed calls, ignored texts, the chaos
- **The audience hears OTHER shop owners describing StyleDesk-relevant pain → product sells itself**
- Becomes the show's signature question — clippable, meme-able, recurring

#### 🎙️ Closer
> *"If you could change ONE thing about running your shop tomorrow — no money limit, no skill required — what would it be?"*

**Why:**
- Reflective + aspirational
- Guests often describe StyleDesk features without knowing it
- Stitch all the answers into a montage later: *"10 shop owners describe what they wish existed → here it is"* — huge organic content

## The spice — rotate 5–7 per episode

Pick from these based on the guest's vibe and your prep.

### Origin / identity
- *How did you get into this?*
- *Who taught you?*
- *Where did the shop name come from?*
- *What was your first shop like vs now?*

### Story-driven
- *What's the wildest client story you've ever had?*
- *Walk me through the worst day you've had at the shop.*
- *Best comeback you've had with a difficult client?*

### Industry truth
- *What's the biggest mistake you see new [barbers/nail techs] making?*
- *What's something everyone in the industry believes that's wrong?*
- *If you had to start over today, what would you do differently?*

### Business / tactical
- *How do you find new clients in 2026?*
- *What's working on social media for you right now?*
- *How do you think about pricing?*
- *How do you handle no-shows?*

### Wish-list *(gold for you)*
- *What do booking apps still get wrong?*
- *If you could hire one role tomorrow, what would it be?*
- *What would your ideal week look like — what's missing?*

This category is intentional. It surfaces unsolved problems. Some guests will describe StyleDesk; others will describe future features you should build. **Free product research from your audience.**

### Plug *(always last)*
- *Where can people book you and follow you?*

## Episode structure

| Order | Section | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Spine — Opener | ~1 min | Let them shine |
| 2 | Spice — story-driven | 2–3 min | Warm them up |
| 3 | **Spine — The Anchor** | 2–4 min | Gold content |
| 4 | Spice — 2–3 questions | 4–6 min | Vary by guest |
| 5 | **Spine — Closer** | 1–2 min | Aspirational note |
| 6 | Spice — Plug | 30 sec | Their handle, their booking link |

**Target full episode:** 8–15 min. Long enough to dig in, short enough to actually finish.

**The Anchor goes early-middle**, not last — gives you room to dig into pain points after they open up. The Closer is last because it leaves the audience aspirational + ready to click your bio.

## Content multiplier — one interview = 4–5 posts

Every interview should generate:
1. **The full episode** (8–15 min, posted as a Reel or carousel of clips)
2. **The Anchor clip** (vertical, 30–60s — the Saturday 2pm answer)
3. **The Closer clip** (vertical, 30–60s — the wish-list answer)
4. **A story moment clip** (vertical, 30–60s — best moment from the spice questions)
5. **A montage opportunity** (after 3+ episodes, stitch all anchor/closer answers into a "what shop owners actually say" reel)

Plan editing for clips at the moment you record. Note the timestamps as you go.

## Guest care (this is how you scale the show)

What every guest gets:
- **Air time for their shop** — handle + booking link in caption + pinned comment
- **Their shop tagged** in your post (their notification = re-share = audience import)
- **The edited episode + 3 vertical clips** sent to them within 48 hours so they can post to their own feed
- **$30 Venmo within 24 hours** of recording (this is reputation insurance — DMV is a small network, word travels)
- **Cross-promotion** — share their content to your Stories at least once that week

If guest has a great episode, **send them a small thank-you gift** (e.g., a $25 Sephora/Ulta card if it's a nail tech, $25 Amazon if it's a barber). Costs you $25 — but they'll recommend you to 5 other techs in their network.

## Prep checklist (the day of recording)

- [ ] Watched 5+ of their recent posts and noted 1 specific reference
- [ ] Picked 5–7 spice questions from the bank, written down in order
- [ ] Audio: AirPods OFF, use the iPhone mic or external mic
- [ ] Lighting: window behind YOU (light on guest), not behind them
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] Test recording 30 seconds before guest joins
- [ ] Send them a Calendly link or text to confirm 30 min before
- [ ] Have the spine questions ON A NOTE in front of you so you don't forget the order

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't pitch StyleDesk during the conversation. Ever.
- ❌ Don't ask "yes/no" questions. Always open-ended.
- ❌ Don't read the spice questions in order — let the conversation flow, pull questions when natural
- ❌ Don't talk over the guest. Long pauses = gold.
- ❌ Don't apologize on camera. Cut it in editing.
- ❌ Don't release without sending the guest the final cut for approval
